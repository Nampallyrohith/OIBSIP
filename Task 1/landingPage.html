Hunger-Free

Extra Food transportation to needy people

A hunger-free society is a state where every individual has access to enough nutritious food, and no one suffers from hunger or malnutrition. It requires policies and programs that address the root causes of hunger, as well as the cooperation of governments, organizations, and individuals. Achieving a hunger-free world is essential for individuals to reach their full potential and for communities to thrive.

Our Initiatives

There are different ways to collect food for donations. One way is to organize a traditional food drive \by collecting nonperishable and unspoiled perishable food from homes and businesses, which can then be donated to food banks, soup kitchens, pantries, and shelters. Another way is to hold a virtual food drive where you can collect online donations that will be used to purchase meals and other services for people facing hunger.

The person in charge of school food service will work with the sanitarian and/or local regulatory authority that conducts food safety inspections to ensure donation procedures are compliant with Wisconsin Food Code. Any recommendations made by the sanitarian and/or local regulatory authority will be implemented. Special attention will be given to time/temperature control safety (TCS) foods.

Donating food to the poor is a crucial step towards addressing hunger and malnutrition among underprivileged communities. There are various organizations like Uday Foundation and Akshaya Patra that work towards providing free, wholesome food to families of underprivileged patients and children who come from far-off places. These organizations rely on the support and contributions of donors who can help them in their mission to feed the needy.
